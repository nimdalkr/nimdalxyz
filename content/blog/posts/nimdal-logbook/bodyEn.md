A portfolio can show outcomes, but it usually hides the thinking that created them. This blog exists to preserve that thinking.

Nimdal is not only a visual identity. It is a working surface for research tools, automation systems, Web3 market notes, game-like product ideas, and the practical campaign work behind them. The homepage can introduce that world quickly. The blog is where the slower material can live.

## What belongs here

- Research notes that are too specific for a landing page.
- Build logs for products, prototypes, and automation systems.
- Career notes from campaign planning, SEO, reporting, and client operations.
- Personal essays about identity, taste, tools, and the way products should feel.

## The rule

The blog should be useful before it is decorative.

The design can carry the Nimdal identity, but the writing has to be clear enough that a reader can take something away from it. If the homepage is the ocean map, the blog is the notebook kept on the desk after the dive.
